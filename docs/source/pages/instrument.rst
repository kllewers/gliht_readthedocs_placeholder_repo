======================
Instrument & Sensors
======================

G-LiHT combines five sensors on a single airborne platform, flown together so
that every measurement is collected over the same ground footprint at the same
time.

Scanning Lidar
===============

* Wavelength: 1550 nm
* Swath width: 387 m
* Measures fine-scale 3D canopy and terrain structure across the full swath

Profiling Lidar
================

* Wavelength: 905 nm
* Collects a high-frequency nadir profile of canopy and surface structure,
  complementing the scanning lidar

Imaging Spectrometer (Hyperspectral)
=====================================

* Spectral range: 420-950 nm (narrowband, ~1.5 nm resolution)
* Acquisition rate: 50 Hz
* Captures vegetation composition and biophysical/biochemical properties

Irradiance Spectrometer
=========================

* Spectral range: 350-1,100 nm
* Acquisition rate: 1 Hz
* Measures incoming solar irradiance to support reflectance calibration of the
  imaging spectrometer

Thermal Camera
================

* Spectral range: 8-14 micrometers (broadband)
* Swath width: 173 m
* Acquisition rate: 25 Hz
* Measures land surface temperature, used to study vegetation function and
  stress

Spatial Resolution
====================

All G-LiHT data products share a common spatial resolution of approximately
1 meter, enabling direct pixel-level fusion of lidar, spectral, and thermal
measurements collected on the same flight.
